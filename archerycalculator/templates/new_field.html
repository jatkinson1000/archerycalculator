This page is a preview of the "new" Archery GB field classifications for iteration of score boundaries and rules prior to final release.

SAP 7.7 - Classification Schemes: Field Archery

  1. The defined year for all field classifications is a calendar year.
  2. Bowstyles. The scheme shall apply to archers shooting bowstyles detailed in Rules 502 (a) and (b) i and iii -- v.
  3. Qualifying Scores: Please refer to the field classification tables (published separately by Archery GB) for the scores required for each bowstyle, gender, age, and round. (all tables are here: https://archerygb.org/resources/find-a-document/awards/)
  4. Archer classifications (3rd, 2nd, 1st)
    1. Qualifying Rounds: As shown in the classifications tables referred to in XXX
    2. Total number of rounds: 3
    3. Standards: Scores may come from practice but must be shot under Archery GB Rules of Shooting.
    4. Claims: Dealt with at club level.
  5. Bowman classifications (3rd, 2nd, 1st)
    1. Qualifying Rounds: As shown in the classifications tables referred to in XXX
    2. Total number of rounds: 3
    3. Standards: All scores must be shot under Archery GB Rules of Shooting, at a meeting organised by Archery GB or a body affiliated to Archery GB, or at any associated club target day (Rules of shooting - Rule 313).
    4. Claims: Dealt with at club level.
  6. Master Bowman classifications (Master Bowman, Grand Master Bowman, Elite Master Bowman)
    1. Qualifying Rounds: As shown in the classifications tables referred to in XXX with the additional requirement that only one score from a single event may be used.
    2. Total number of rounds: 3
    3. Standards: All rounds must be shot at events organised by WA, WA Members or Archery GB, or at an Arrowhead Status or UK Record Status meeting.
    4. Claims: Dealt with by Membership Services as detailed in 3b.
